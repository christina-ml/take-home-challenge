Fun Fact!

Not only am I interested in learning how to code, I'm also a textile designer! I am involved in the whole process from creating trends, color palettes, choosing artwork from art studios or creating original artwork of my own to put onto products. Then the new designs get sent off to factories overseas, and they print the designs onto fabrics or weave them, and turn them into products you find around your home:

  • Bedding, sheets, decorative pillows
  • throw blankets
  • window curtains
  • shower curtains, towels, bath rugs
  • and more!
This is all done on the computer using Adobe Photoshop and Illustrator.

Fun Fact #2!

I am an artist, so I also like to do crafts and knit. I've knit all sorts of things! I've made: scarves, hats, sweaters, a vest, mittens, wrist mitts, pillow covers, a teapot mitten, and much more! Recently, I've been making a few shawls! They take a very long time, but they are so warm. During COVID-19 I learned how to use a cable needle, and made a hat with cables. Looks amazing!
Also really getting into color changing patterns such as fair isle, where I now have 3 hats from Shetland Wool Week. So much fun, so much yarn, and of course there's always YouTube videos to help for difficult knitting techniques such as: make 1 right, and make 1 left (triangular shawls tend to use these every other row). The stitches will be leaning towards either the right, or the left! Increases, decreases, and fixing mistakes is all part of it. Sometimes you even find a hole where you've accidentally dropped a stitch and need to go find a crochet hook to loop it back in. Kind of like de-bugging code when you get an error, but with yarn.
